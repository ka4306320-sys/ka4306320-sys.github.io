History

History of Statistics

From ancient census records to modern machine learning ‚Äî 300+ years of statistical development.

Ancient Times

Census & Record Keeping

Ancient Egypt, China, and Rome collected population data for taxation and military purposes. This was humanity's first systematic data collection.

1662

John Graunt ‚Äî Birth of Statistics

John Graunt published the first statistical analysis of mortality data in London ‚Äî identifying patterns in births, deaths, and disease. He founded modern demography.

1713

Bernoulli ‚Äî Law of Large Numbers

Jacob Bernoulli proved that as sample size increases, the sample mean approaches the true population mean ‚Äî the fundamental basis for all statistical sampling.

1763

Bayes' Theorem

Thomas Bayes introduced conditional probability ‚Äî the foundation of Bayesian statistics that treats probability as a degree of belief updated with evidence.

1809

Gauss ‚Äî Normal Distribution

Carl Friedrich Gauss developed the method of least squares and described the famous bell curve ‚Äî the most important probability distribution in statistics.

1900

Karl Pearson ‚Äî Modern Methods

Karl Pearson formalized the correlation coefficient and chi-squared test ‚Äî founding modern mathematical statistics as a formal academic discipline.

1925

Fisher ‚Äî Hypothesis Testing

Ronald Fisher introduced null hypothesis testing, p-values, and ANOVA ‚Äî revolutionizing experimental science. Still the dominant framework in modern statistics.

2000s

Big Data & Machine Learning

Statistics merged with computer science to create data science. Neural networks, random forests, and deep learning ‚Äî all built on statistical foundations.

Today

The Age of Data

Statistical methods power Google search, Netflix, medical research, climate models, and AI. Statistics is the backbone of the modern digital world.
